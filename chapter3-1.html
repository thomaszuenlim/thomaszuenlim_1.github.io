Chapter 3.1

Paper Prototyping: Development, Testing and Evaluation

For paper prototype version 1, we decided to present the primary features to the user in order to gain insights into what seemed familiar and what was miscommunicated. The primary goals here were to determine if we were on the right track and get the big glaring issues out of the way first as much as possible.

Due to some screens that we felt could be designed in separate ways, we decided to use methods like A/B testing to see which versions users preferred or possible elements they would like combined. This was also used in conjunction with usability testing, with ourselves moderating and observing what our users were doing while going through the application.

We should also note that our paper prototyping had initially began as hand drawn sketches, which we then moved to Balsamic to create wireframes. However, we felt that there were far more screens that we wanted to test, and hence we moved the creation of wireframes into Sketch in order to have the tools to be able to create enough screens that would better represent this application and its functions for version 1 testing.

These wireframes were still printed out on paper for users to physically interact with.

Onboarding 1.1.x

In this onboarding process, the user is taken through a setup process for the financial accounts they might be interested in adding. This is a major part of the application being able to support users throughout their lifecycle, as financial priorities change over time.

The main usability test for Paper Prototype was aimed primarily at ensuring that the entire process was simple and straightforward.

Feedback



    Onboarding

  • 1.1.1
    They are not sure whether they can select multiple accounts or not on the choosing account page. It often was interpreted as a dropdown.

  • 1.1.2
    On the choosing account page, the + sign seems like it is going to expand and show more information, rather than selecting accounts.

  • 1.1.3
    Pagination dots don’t make much sense in the onboarding process, since people assume that they can swipe between setup accounts, as opposed to being informed that there are X number of accounts to set up

  • 1.1.4
    Some back or cancel options in this process couldn’t be immediately found

  • 1.1.5
    General grey levels of the paper prototype tend to miscommunicate functionality, like some users thought that the greyed out ‘custom’ account meant that they could scroll down, as opposed to a constrained button.

Justification



    Onboarding

  • 1.1.1
    This is a feature that needs to be changed, as it is a key component for enabling users to add multiple accounts
    (Change made for Paper Prototype V2)

  • 1.1.2
    This UI element has to change, consider radio buttons, which are universally understood as a multiple selection process
    (Change made for Paper Prototype V2)

  • 1.1.3
    This will be removed and we will need to rethink how we communicate the idea that the user has x number of accounts left to setup
    (Change made for Paper Prototype V2)

  • 1.1.4
    The grey that was used in the paper prototypes seemed okay digitally, but unfortunately didn’t transfer over to paper prototypes as successfully. However, this could also be a great safety precaution for the final digital product, and perhaps we should make the greys darker
    (Change made for Paper Prototype V2)

  • 1.1.5
    Similarly to the issue presented in 1.1.4, and will be fixed with the same increase in grey. However, it did lead us to ask ourselves why the custom would be there. Initially, the idea was that if you couldn’t find your chosen account in the list, you could add your own; however, open banking support must be available with that chosen account. Thus, we felt it would make more sense in this assessment to remove the ‘custom’ option, and instead present the list of account vendors who are most likely to have open banking support, with others added when they enable it.
    (Change made for Paper Prototype V2)


Pay 1.2.x

In the Pay page, users are able to make mobile purchases with cards linked to the application, a digital copy of the one issued by their banks. There are 3 variations that were A/B tested here, primarily around how budget would be visualised as well as the addition of transactional data.

Feedback



    Pay

  • 1.2.1
    They want to see the balance of budget before they pay to remind themselves how much budget left and save more money if it is possible

  • 1.2.2
    The colour coded card does not look like it is indicating how much budget left, but whether the card transaction was completed or not. For example, the green card seems it is approved, yellow seems pending, the red seems declined

  • 1.2.3
    They want to know what happen if the user spent all the budget

  • 1.2.4
    The colour coded circle system should be reversed. The current system is that the colour indicator is filling up from zero to 100%, which is from green to red, but the user is not adding money, but using money. Therefore, the circle gauge should be starting from full colour, which is green to little gauge, which is red colour

  • 1.2.5
    They want to see how many days left of budget that they set as well

  • 1.2.6
    Where did the user set up the budget for the budgeting circle in the pay screen?

  • 1.2.7
    Most users preferred to see the budget circle when asked to compare between the 3 different designs, and while it would be nice to have the transactional data, was something that didn’t really make much sense as the user would be standing at the point of purchase when they see it

  • 1.2.8
    Users need to be able to choose from different cards on the Pay page

  • 1.2.9
    Greatly enjoys the instant feedback that they receive as soon as they make a purchase, that way they know instantly where they’re budget is at. This is something that one user expressed as ‘absent’ in the core Apple Pay functionality

  • 1.2.10
    Do all the back buttons on the Pay screen make sense, like perhaps when you have already made a payment, should the back button still be there? Or maybe the current languaging of ‘Close’ to go back to the main pay screen after making a card payment needs to change?

Justification



    Pay

  • 1.2.1
    Will need to implement a design that allows users to see the current balance of their cards before they make a purchase; i.e. on the initial pay screen
    (Change make for Paper Prototype V2)

  • 1.2.2
    May end up just removing the colour coded card design as it also tends to cause users more stress than intended; and with your personal finances, we should be aiming to reduce it
    (Change made for Paper Prototype V2)

  • 1.2.3
    Budget circle indicator may just continue to be red, but start a new circle with a changed dialog from ‘Left to Spend’ to ‘Over Spend’ (Considered but not required to be designed for the moment)

  • 1.2.4
    Budget circle needs to be changed to count down from 100% as opposed to the current design that counts up from 0%
    (Change made for Paper Prototype V2)

  • 1.2.5
    This is something to consider for a more fully fledged payment and budgeting design but may be implemented at a later point in time. In consideration, we found that this could overcomplicate the pay screen, and was not implemented for the future iterations

  • 1.2.6
    We need to have interactions in the onboarding process so users can set up an initial budget for the cards they have
    (Change made for Paper Prototype V2)

  • 1.2.7
    General consensus points towards preferring the budget circle, so this will be the direction forward. Additionally, while there is some evidence of some testers wanting both the budget circle and transactional data, the designs for this were far too busy
    (Change made for Paper Prototype V2)

  • 1.2.8
    This needs to be implemented, users will have multiple cards that are going to be able to be connected to this app.
    (Change made for Paper Prototype V2)

  • 1.2.9
    We need to show users that they can choose from multiple cards to pay with, especially if they have multiple bank accounts.
    (Change made for Paper Prototype V2)

  • 1.2.10
    Not entirely confident in the justification for this change, however, due to the additional phase of paper prototyping, we could change the language of the return to pay screen to something like ‘Done’ this as an A/B test and see if it causes any issues?
    (Change made for Paper Prototype V2)


Spend 1.3.x

In the spending page, users can view more information related to their cards. This includes information like balance, budget limits and recent transactions. There are two variations in this version, primarily aimed towards how users would like to view this information and how they would get there. One is a single page scrolling design, and the other is a split version, where they select the card they want to view information about.

Feedback



    Spend

  • 1.3.1
    The split Track screen variant has one too many steps for the user to be able to view their card transactional data

  • 1.3.2
    The card screen resembles the Pay section screen, which makes a user confused

  • 1.3.3
    Most users prefer the list view for viewing recent transactions as opposed to the swiping view. This is because it allows users to do quick mental maths as they read down the list of transactions they’ve made. They can also view more transactions at once

  • 1.3.4
    Since there is the budget limit present on the spending page, can the user tap on it as a shortcut to get to viewing their budget?

  • 1.3.5
    Some users expressed that they preferred the view of the one page Spending screen, but liked the visual cue for scrolling that was evident in the split Spending screens design

  • 1.3.6
    Do I need a separate screen to view all my cards just to have the additional option to choose which card I want to pay with on the spend screen? The Spending page variant is a bit useless

  • 1.3.7
    How many transactions is considered to be ‘recent’? I.e. how will scrolling work, will it only show a set amount? Or will there be an automatic loading of more transactions when I swipe down too far? Or will there be a read more?

Justification



    Spend

  • 1.3.1
    Most users expressed that they preferred the single screen design where the user is able to view all of the card spending data at once, and this will be the direction forward
    (Change made for Paper Prototype V2)

  • 1.3.2
    We felt that with the current design, the additional data below the relevant card in the spending screen communicated that the user was indeed on another section of the app.
    (Unchanged)

  • 1.3.3
    While there were one or two testers who liked the split view where you have to choose the card first to see more information, we believe that the general consensus points towards the one page design and this seems to be the best direction forward
    (Change made for Paper Prototype V2)

  • 1.3.4
    This behaviour should definitely be something we implement as it could possibly turn into a great opportunity for power users to change certain aspects of the application very quickly
    (Change made for Paper Prototype V2)

  • 1.3.5
    There is a behaviour that is present in the split view which we can carry across in our one page chosen direction. By providing a small sliver of the next card on the left or right of the current card, we can still utilise this subconscious behaviour to ensure that users can safely assume that they can swipe across to another card
    (Change made for Paper Prototype V2)

  • 1.3.6
    This feedback further supports the one page direction, as it doesn’t seem too necessary to enable the user to choose a card from the spending page to pay with. It is essentially just having the pay screen within the spending screen.
    (Change made for Paper Prototype V2)

  • 1.3.7
    Upon thinking that perhaps 3-5 days may be a good timeframe, we’ve realised that one person could very possibly make hundreds of transactions within that timespan. Hence, we’ve changed our course of thinking to be based on the number of transactions; i.e. perhaps shown a limit of ten? Additionally, it doesn’t seem like it would be overly necessary to have a separate page for viewing more transactions and for now, we’d most likely stay with 10 as default, but allow users to load more when they reach the end.
    (Change made for Paper Prototype V2)


Track 1.4.x

The track page is where users can view all the financial activity that may occur in the financial accounts they have chosen to access through the application. It was important that users feel like all of their accounts are transparent and readily viewable, like most recent activity or even analytical information. The A/B testing involved two variations of the track screen, one being a single page scrolling design, and the other with all your accounts having their own page that they could swipe left and right to get to.

Feedback



    Track

  • 1.4.1
    For the swiping accounts version, users weren’t aware that they could swipe between the different accounts because the pagination dots were in the top right corner

  • 1.4.2
    The labels for the different tiles are confusing, as I might have multiple bank accounts which means that the tiles might end up being labelled the same

  • 1.4.3
    When a user hit the card to see detailed information, the word ‘Banking’ goes from left to right and it is confusing

  • 1.4.4
    They want to see a page where every information is summarised and see it at a glance

  • 1.4.5
    The label next to the Track in the top right is slightly confusing, should it be so readily available?

  • 1.4.6
    Some graphs are less useful than others for different accounts, like a weekly view for superannuation would be useless; perhaps tailored graph views for different accounts (like quarterly for superannuation)?

  • 1.4.7
    Perhaps a small percentage letting me know that maybe I am doing better or worse than last time period? i.e. ‘You’re earning 5% more than this time last year?’

  • 1.4.8
    Pagination icons for swiping between accounts doesn’t make much sense in the top right corner

  • 1.4.9
    When tapping a tile to view more information, will the expanding tile push the other tiles away or slide down on top of the other accounts?

  • 1.4.10
    One could begin to forget which account is next with swiping between accounts on the Track page

  • 1.4.11
    Would like the swiping between accounts, but wasn’t a fan of how the main tile information was changed to look more like it was part of the background and no longer looked like a tile

  • 1.4.12
    Would the user be able to see better graph information, like being able to tap on a specific datapoint? This could make the yearly graph more useful

  • 1.4.13
    If I am in the graph view of an account, can I swipe left or right to go to the next consecutive account but stay in graph view?

Justification



    Track

  • 1.4.1
    If we decide to go with the swiping between accounts Track variation, we need to make it clearer to the user that they can swipe between the different accounts
    (Change made for Paper Prototype V2)

  • 1.4.2
    Definitely considered and will need to be implemented, users need to be able to know exactly which account they are looking at, even more so when it comes to their finances
    (Change made for High Fidelity V1)

  • 1.4.3
    May need to remove the labels that were designed to let the user know what account they are currently viewing when entering the ‘more info’ view. It is slightly confusing because users are only one level away from the main track screen
    (Change made for Paper Prototype V2)

  • 1.4.4
    This is interesting because what has been described is essentially the one page Track screen with scrolling, assuming that they have only a few accounts to track which means that there would be no scrolling. However, scrolling is inevitably going to happen as more accounts are added, like several different accounts under one bank account, which would ultimately result in the same thing.
    (Unchanged)

  • 1.4.5
    Being able to add a new financial account isn’t a function that needs to be so readily available, and hence may need to be moved to the settings page.
    (Change made for Paper Prototype V2)

  • 1.4.6
    This issue was a difficult one to justify. On one hand, we agree that some graph views are going to be more useful than others, like a daily graph for investments vs superannuation and hence perhaps we should indeed have individually created graph controls. However, in consideration of the possible development of such an application, it is important to consider what is possible as a designer as opposed to a developer. Rather, we feel that from a developer’s view, creating one library for financial graphs to use across all accounts would greatly reduce application memory and usage requirements. For this reason, we’ve decided to continue with the current design that has the same graph view controls across all financial accounts
    (Unchanged)

  • 1.4.7
    Implementing features like this were part of other design concepts prior to this application, however, were ultimately left out in this initial design due to the increased complexity and density of information being presented to the user. We do agree that this is something that the application should have, since all the data is there, but for now is something we may push to the backlog in order to focus on improving what is currently there
    (Backlogged)

  • 1.4.8
    Pagination dots that indicate the number of accounts a user may have tracked will need to move to a more accessible and viewable position.
    (Change made for Paper Prototype V2)

  • 1.4.9
    This is something to consider for the high fidelity stage, but we feel it might make more sense as an ‘overlay’ and not push the other tiles out but this behaviour could change by the time we reach high fidelity
    (Change made for High Fidelity V2)

  • 1.4.10
    Linked to the pagination dots, perhaps we need to include the names of the accounts in a more prominent position, in addition to utilising greys to show the user that they can get to that account
    (Change made for Paper Prototype V2)

  • 1.4.11
    In moving forward with the swiping between accounts, we could do a hybrid of the two where we keep the tile setup, but can swipe between the different financial accounts horizontally
    (Change made for Paper Prototype V2)

  • 1.4.12
    This is a feature we should indeed implement, however, we decided that this could be better communicated in the high fidelity stage
    (Change made for High Fidelity V1)

  • 1.4.13
    This could be very interesting behaviour to have, however, there are some financial accounts that may only have one tile to view more of. Additionally, in keeping with the idea of removing the labels for 1.4.3, we may need to restrict such behaviour to swiping only between tiles of the same account otherwise this could be very confusing.
    (Unchanged)


Notifications & Settings 1.5.x

As more ‘minor’ features of the application, notifications and settings are intended more to round out the application. Being notified of immediate changes to any of your financial accounts is very important behaviour and it currently reflects this. There is also an A/B test for the settings icon, as we decided to look into this small part of the user flow. We wanted to compare if users would prefer to have to get to their profile through settings or vice versa.

Feedback



    Notifications & Settings

  • 1.5.1
    The settings options might need some separators between them as they could be too close to each other

  • 1.5.2
    What happens when I decide to reset my account? Will there be a confirmation page? But also why is there a log out button and a reset button?

  • 1.5.3
    Is there a limit to the number of notifications I can see?

  • 1.5.4
    Maybe reset account to remove accounts is a bit unintuitive, perhaps put this in the linked accounts settings option?

  • 1.5.5
    The profile icon doesn’t make too much sense as most users preferred the user flow to go settings -> profile, as opposed to profile -> settings. Additionally, the icon for settings tends to look like a ‘sort by’ button

Justification



    Notifications & Settings

  • 1.5.1
    Settings options are buttons, and hence will need to have button features in order to communicate this. We believe this will satisfy this requirement
    (Change made for High Fidelity V1)

  • 1.5.2
    Having both reset and logout caused major confusion with our testers, and hence we should just keep logout and remove reset
    (Change made for Paper Prototype V2)

  • 1.5.3
    We wanted to keep notifications familiar to the end user, and hence have decided to keep notifications coming, but allow them to delete them
    (Change made for High Fidelity V2)

  • 1.5.4
    By removing ‘reset’ in 1.5.2 and considering 1.4.5, the behaviour for adding or removing new accounts will be moved into the ‘Linked Accounts’ section under Profile in the settings menu

  • 1.5.5
    This is something we can still continue to test, as it isn’t a decision that is too important at the moment. We do feel that it is safe to assume that the user flow will be settings -> profile, and perhaps we may enquire about other icon possibilities
    (Change made for High Fidelity V1)



Paper Prototype Version 1: Reflection


In reflection on the Paper Prototype version 1 usability testing, we quickly realised that there is much more to budgeting that previously realised. There is a level of depth that seems to be the major reason why so many budgeting apps exist.

It made us consider whether we were aiming to achieve too much with this mobile application, especially when so many other budgeting apps do one thing, but do them incredibly well with immense depth.
