Chapter 1.4

Design Concepts: Benefits and Drawbacks

Lydian Money is a combination of two of the previous design concepts. It is a mobile application that allows the user to view all of their financial accounts in one platform, whether that be your personal banking accounts, superannuation, managed funds, Afterpay, etc.

Lydian grows with its user, throughout all their twists and turns in life. The modular interface enables users to pick and choose which financial accounts mean the most to them at any given time. Interested in investing but not your superannuation for the time being? Lydian supports you by ensuring you get to pick what matters to you; when it matters to you. As you progress through your own life cycle, Lydian continues to provide the ability to tailor your financial journey.

This application will become the primary way that users can interact with all of their financial activities, helping shed a light on what might be hidden from you. Through the open banking protocol, financial institutions can allow you to authorise your financial data to be accessed securely within Lydian.

Additionally, features like paying through the mobile payments is supported, with additional feedback like a traffic light system that provides instant visual feedback, letting you know immediately if your spending pattern is off.

We decided to proceed with this concept for Lydian Money, as it directly addressed the key issues that we identified, with reducing financial stress by helping make all your financial activity more transparent, in addition to a financial platform that can grow with you as your financial priorities change.
