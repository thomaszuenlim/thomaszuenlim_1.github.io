Getting Started in Adobe Illustrator



1. The Welcome Dashboard

Welcome to Adobe Illustrator! The first screen you'll see upon opening up the program is the Welcome Dashboard! This features your most recent Illustrator projects!

2. List or thumbnail view

List view can be a bit lacking in its visuals - we recommend going into the thumbnail view, which can be accessed as shown on the right.

3. New Document

The important part! The dimensions and settings for your new document sets the stage for what you're creating. From the basic menu, you can decide on your canvas dimensions, units, orientation, artboards and bleed settings for printing! (don't worry these can be changed even after making the document!)

4. Advanced Document Options

Here's where you can define what colour space to work in (RGB for digital and CMYK for print); additionally you can change your pixel density. If you are designing something for print - you would usually use 300ppi as opposed to 72ppi for purely digital media.

5. The Dashboard

Here's where you'll spend 90% of your time in Illustrator! We'll cover the primary features of this dashboard below!

6. Workspaces

Workspaces allow you to alter the layout of tools based on the templates provided - but you also have the ability to make and save custom ones! (Like I have here!)

7. The Tools

We like to define the left side of Illustrator as our 'Tools'. This is where we'll find the tools that allow us to create something new; like shapes or text!

8. The Properties

We define the right side of Illustrator as our 'Properties'. This is where you can change the properties of selected elements, like colour, typpography and more!

Congratulations on completing the Getting Started Guide to Adobe Illustrator!
