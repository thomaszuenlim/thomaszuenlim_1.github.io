Chapter 3.2

Paper Prototyping: Development, Testing and Evaluation

Moving into version 2 of the paper prototyping phase, there are several main features that we aim to get further insight into. This is the first prototype that features budgeting, in addition to sign up and sign in and tutorial screens at the end of the onboarding process







Sign up & Onboarding 2.1.x

This section now has a new feature implemented, the register and sign in functionality. There are also new tutorial screens added, with budgeting support. Additionally, there were several changes made from Paper Prototype V1.

Feedback



    Sign up & Onboarding

  • 2.1.1
    Doesn’t understand Register or Sign in with Apple

  • 2.1.2
    Usually user gets an email confirmation after they sign up, but there’s none here

  • 2.1.3
    Design still doesn’t clearly communicate that you can select multiple accounts. The new open circles look more like radio buttons, which further indicates that only one option is available

  • 2.1.4
    In the tutorial screens, while there are pagination dots, having a ‘next’ button would still be nice to have

  • 2.1.5
    What if I have two bank accounts?

  • 2.1.6
    What if I only want to budget for certain categories and not the set ones? Or perhaps I can just add a custom category? Add or remove budget categories?

  • 2.1.7
    For register, language should change to ‘Sign up with Apple’ as opposed to ‘Sign in with Apple’

  • 2.1.8
    Initial budget setup should have a blank field for the budget value, otherwise users may feel confused as to why it is pre-filled

  • 2.1.9
    Could average category data still be available to the user in the budget setup if it was able to be sourced from the bank accounts that are linked?

  • 2.1.10
    Users thought that the ‘Set budget categories’ text was the button to allow users to add a custom category

Justification



    Sign up & Onboarding

  • 2.1.1
    Sign in with Apple is unfamiliar to many of the users we tested with, Sign in with Google is far more universally understood
    (Change made for High Fidelity V1)

  • 2.1.2
    We need to communicate the full features of the Sign in and Register options
    (Change made for High Fidelity V1)

  • 2.1.3
    As this is now the second time this has been brought up, we need to reconsider how we present additional options to the user. Additionally, we had feedback from one user who would not prefer squared boxes since it conflicted with the overall design language of the application. Perhaps there is a way we can skip either of these and come back to the fundamentals?
    (Change made for High Fidelity V1)

  • 2.1.4
    This should be implemented, we cannot assume that all users are comfortable with swiping between the different pages of the tutorial screen
    (Change made for High Fidelity V1)

  • 2.1.5
    While we’ve almost solved the approach for multiple accounts, we’ve now realised we need to approach this in a ‘micro’ approach. Many users will have multiple bank accounts and we need to accommodate for this
    (Change made for High Fidelity V1)

  • 2.1.6
    There are several levels of user interaction in this issue. One, for custom categories, that is something we can implement with ease in the next design. However, we may need to consider pushing adding or removing budget categories into the backlog, as it is a feature that may introduce new functionality
    (Change made for High Fidelity V1)

  • 2.1.7
    The language for registering an account should be reflected in the signing up options, this must be implemented
    (Change made for High Fidelity V1)

  • 2.1.8
    This is possible an error on our part. We weren’t careful enough to remove the value in the budget setup process and this will be reflected in the next iteration
    (Change made for High Fidelity V1)

  • 2.1.9
    This is quite interesting, and is something we wholeheartedly agree on. We should present the user with the data that we have been provided with, it is the responsibility of the application to do so, especially in the name of transparency. However, for now, this seems to be more of a backend requirement, in addition to working with the financial account services to ensure this behaviour
    (Unchanged)

  • 2.1.10
    Following from the changes proposed in 2.1.6 this is now sufficiently provided. We will remove this confusing line of text from this screen
    (Change made for High Fidelity V1)


Pay 2.2.x

The Pay screen received many changes, primarily found in the initial screen, as well as the direction that it took on from the feedback in paper prototype 1. With added multiple card support and a cleaner budgeting visualisation, we hope that it provides further usability to the end user.

Feedback



    Pay

  • 2.2.1
    Budget decreases from 100%, but the phrasing as ‘left to spend’ indicates that the colours should be flipped

  • 2.2.2
    Can both information, which is how much budget left and how much money just spent, show at the same time?

  • 2.2.3
    Can we change the colour changing tiers for what percentage of my budget means green, yellow or red?

  • 2.2.4
    What happens when you click ‘Done’? Maybe ‘Close’ will work better for ending the payment stage

  • 2.2.5
    One user expressed that there are several occasions when a vendor may not have set up their point of sale (POS) system with their business name, and hence could result in some transactions in a bank statement not accurately reflecting the purchase. For this reason, they expressed that they would like to have the transaction information back in the pay screen when they make the payment so they could possibly correct this behaviour by editing it?

Justification



    Pay

  • 2.2.1
    Budget circle is still not represented correctly, even though it now counts down from 100%, the portion that is coloured is incorrect and needs to be flipped around
    (Change made for High Fidelity V1)

  • 2.2.2
    Users are familiar with the current position of the ‘left to spend’ dialog and this is associated with the budget circle. However, the current budget circle doesn’t clearly communicate this since the colours are flipped so this could cause some to initially misunderstand what it represents (the amount left). Additionally, we included the amount just spent in the same box for continued familiarity
    (Unchanged)

  • 2.2.3
    This is user interaction that should be implemented, however, is a level of customizability that would introduce some new features into the application. While we would love to implement this into the design, we felt as though it was something to put into the backlog
    (Backlog)

  • 2.2.4
    This is an interesting back and forth, but it was clear that our paper prototype v2 testers felt more strongly about this change, and hence we have decided to return to the ‘Close’ button language for the end of the payment process
    (Change made for High Fidelity V1)

  • 2.2.5
    The POS transactional information would provide a great level of recognition and recall for the user, while also possibly providing a depth of information that could potentially solve the problem identified. This is something to put into the backlog, as it extends the current design into developing new features
    (Backlog)


Spend 2.3.x

At first glance, the spending page hasn’t taken on the greatest amount of cosmetic changes, but there are subtle interaction clues that have been derived from the previous A/B testing variations, like the horizontal scrolling hint. Additionally, features like the budget circle from the pay screen have also been included.

Feedback



    Spend

  • 2.3.1
    One user suggested maybe including a scroll bar to better indicate that the recent transaction list is scrollable

  • 2.3.2
    Show how much percentage of the budget left as well.

  • 2.3.3
    If I swipe at the bottom of the screen, does that still swipe across my navigation?

  • 2.3.4
    Being able to view transactions filtered by category could be a very useful feature

Justification



    Spend

  • 2.3.1
    We could include a scrollbar to better indicate that you can scroll in your recent transactions, however, scroll bars tend to be more of a web-based visual aid that isn’t primarily present in touch-screen based applications. Additionally, mobile scrolling is a very familiar behaviour for most users. We also had many other users express that they knew it was scrollable even before wanting to play around with the application due to the bottom cutoff.
    (Unchanged)

  • 2.3.2
    This is something to consider, however, we do recognise that we still have the budget circle present in the card of the Spending screen, which we feel presents a better visual aid for budget progress as opposed to a percentage, since it provides a relative position from an immediate glance.
    (Unchanged)

  • 2.3.3
    While this behaviour may be intuitive, we’ve decided that swiping left and right to navigate between the different features of the app (Pay, Spend, Track) would produce complications and conflict with other swiping left and right interactions, like the pay screen
    (Unchanged)

  • 2.3.4
    This is functionality that could provide a lot of value for the user, and will be considered in the next iteration
    (Backlog)

Notifications & Settings 2.5.x

Notifications didn’t receive a lot of negative feedback from earlier, so one of the goals of this testing session is to explore how notifications could be more user friendly. Additionally, we continued to A/B test the settings flow and relevant navigation bar icon.

Feedback



    Notifications & Settings

  • 2.5.1
    Notifications screen could be considered like an email client. Is there ‘mark as read’ or archive? Or maybe just a way to split up my old notifications from my new ones?

  • 2.5.2
    Either settings icon isn’t superbly clear, perhaps the classic cog icon?

  • 2.5.3
    Unread ‘red dot’ in the nav bar icon when there is a new notification; how does that clear?

  • 2.5.4
    The icon for the notifications bell is the same in both the navigation bar and the settings menu. This leads users to believe that they both go to the notifications page, but rather, the notifications option in the settings menu is for ‘notifications settings’

Justification



    Notifications & Settings

  • 2.5.1
    When considering our notifications, we looked at two slightly different approaches. One can be found in our standard iOS notifications behaviour, where notifications continue to pile in the list, but there is a subtle ‘New’ and ‘Older’ organisation via a no label section for new and ‘notification center’ for older, read notifications. The second can be found in the way that Facebook handles notifications. Users receive a notification badge number, but unread notifications continue to have a slight colour shift to let the user know that they haven’t clicked on the notification yet. Between these two, we’ve felt that the depth of our application’s notifications don’t quite reach the level of Facebook, and hence will be looking towards something closer to ‘New’ and ‘Older’
    (Change made for High Fidelity V1)

  • 2.5.2
    A cog or gear icon seems to be the clear consensus of the universal settings icon, and hence we will be continuing with that
    (Change made for High Fidelity V1)

  • 2.5.3
    Following on from behaviour we researched in 2.5.1, we’ve decided to take a hybrid approach. Hence the behaviour here would provide users with a notification badge to alert them of a new notification, but by returning to any other feature in the app via the navigation bar, will clear the unread badge on the notifications icon
    (Change made for High Fidelity V2)

  • 2.5.4
    This is something we didn’t pick up on our own but is very important to the user’s cognitive load and expectation. This could be solved with a language change in the settings menu from ‘Notifications’ to ‘Notification Settings’
    (Change made for High Fidelity V1)


Budgeting 2.6.x

Budgeting features were not implemented in paper prototype 1, but it was clear that its absence was causing some confusion when it came to seeing budget limits in the pay screens of the previous iteration. Hence, one of the major focuses of paper prototype 2 was to begin to explore the depth of budgeting that our users would expect as part of an integrated financial solution. There was some A/B testing provided here, in order to test how users would like to be presented with features like scrolling through budgeting categories.

Feedback



    Budgeting

  • 2.6.1
    For viewing budget, it current is phrased as ‘spent overall’ which could be slightly confusing - possibly because it is currently a very round number

  • 2.6.2
    Ordering of the view budget categories needs to either be in alphabetical order or dollar amount. Or maybe something the user can choose?

  • 2.6.3
    Should the scrolling of the budget categories show or hide any information to create more viewing space?

  • 2.6.4
    Circle for the budget categories is very confusing - is it a proportion of the overall spending, overall budget, or category budget?

  • 2.6.5
    Maybe add a breakdown of the budget so we can see how much of the budget was made up of a certain category

  • 2.6.6
    Is this viewing the budget for all the cards? Or are we able to individually control card budgets?

  • 2.6.7
    Users also want to be able to see what they actually budgeted for each individual category

  • 2.6.8
    Should users be able to easily change their budget at any time? Perhaps there should be restrictions for this behaviour?

Justification



    Budgeting

  • 2.6.1
    ‘Spent overall’ isn’t as clear as possible, even though we might have been a bit lazy and kept the spent value at $5800. However, we can still make language changes here to make it clearer, perhaps ‘Total Spend’?
    (Change made for High Fidelity V1)

  • 2.6.2
    This is an important feature to include, especially for easier viewing. On first impressions, we felt that organising the budget category view by dollar amount would provide more value to the user as opposed to alphabetical order, so we have decided to go with that for now, with the possibility of a switcher in the future
    (Change made for High Fidelity V1)

  • 2.6.3
    While we could implement this behaviour, there is information present in the overall time period summary which we feel is important information to have on the same screen, especially for comparing budget category amounts to the total budget
    (Unchanged)

  • 2.6.4
    In light of 2.6.4, 2.6.5 and 2.6.7, these three issues can be solved together. In conjunction with 2.6.1, we can use the extra white space to add in the amount budgeted, in addition to a pie chart that had colour coded segments to reflect associated budget categories. To assist with this, the circles to the left of the budget categories will become icons, and there could be an additional line of text below the category amount which reflects the category budget
    (Change made for High Fidelity V1)

  • 2.6.5
    See 2.6.4

  • 2.6.6
    We should have a multi card selector on the screen so that users can switch between the card they want to view or set the budget of
    (Change made for High Fidelity V1)

  • 2.6.7
    See 2.6.4

  • 2.6.8
    This is something we considered when first implementing budgeting into the application. We like the idea of the application enforcing users to stick to their budget and even considered maybe having a password you have to input to change it. However, we decided to move this to the backlog as this wasn’t fully worked out in time
    (Backlog)


Paper Prototype Version 2: Reflection


Following the feedback and plans from the paper prototype 2, there is a little bit more nuance to consider within budgeting. The introduction to the tutorial screen was received well, in addition to the initial budget setup.

Moving forward into high fidelity, we do feel like the amount of testing that we’ve been able to conduct will greatly help in reducing usability issues that may arise in the next iteration.
