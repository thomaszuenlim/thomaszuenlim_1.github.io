Chapter 3.3

High Fidelity Prototyping: Development, Testing and Evaluation

Moving into high fidelity prototypes for versions 3 and 4, the biggest cosmetic change was the addition of the application theme colours and patterns Additionally, every element that was part of the paper prototype took on more colour and depth to better communicate interactions as a whole. This process began in Sketch, designing each of the screens at a high fidelity level, while then moving on to creating simple interactive prototypes in Pop for version 3 testing, and finally moving this into more complex and custom interactions completed in Principle for version 4. By adding this level of user interactivity, version 3 and 4 saw a major shift in our usability testing, as we started to introduce our users to think aloud methods as we gave them tasks to complete. This proved to help immensely in measuring the response times for completing tasks, as well as letting users begin to feel more in control, as though there were no limitations to what they could play with within the prototype.






High Fidelity Prototype: Version 1

Feedback and Justification


Sign up & Onboarding 3.1.x

Feedback



    Sign up & Onboarding

  • 3.1.1
    Current user flow requires users to select all the accounts they want to add first, and then go into the setup process of all the accounts. This user flow may be required to change due to the additional feature of being able to add duplicate financial accounts (like 2 or more personal banking accounts). Some testers expressed that they would prefer to just tap on an account, then go straight into setting up, that way they don’t need to decide on the accounts they want to add all at once

  • 3.1.2
    For signing in with Google, should that sign in path only be available to returning users?

Justification



    Sign up & Onboarding

  • 3.1.1
    This is a major wireflow redesign, but implementing this does seem to make a lot of the onboarding clearer. Additionally, it does make more sense to let the user add and then setup the account they’ve chosen sequentially, adding one account at a time.
    (Changed for High Fidelity V2)

  • 3.1.2
    Signing in with Google should be an option for all users, since we can then utilise the ease-of-use provided by Google
    (Unchanged)


Pay 3.2.x

Feedback



    Pay

  • 3.2.1
    One of the testers mentioned that the pay screen is too complicated and hard to understand the circle budget limit and it’s visually not appealing

  • 3.2.2
    There should be a shortcut to make the payment happen right away. Finding the app, clicking the app, selecting the card can be really annoying if they want to pay instantly

Justification



    Pay

  • 3.2.1
    This user provided some fairly important regarding the pay screen as ‘too complicated’, however, we failed to press on and understand her frame of mind, i.e. perhaps she was unfamiliar with mobile banking in general, and hence this was her initial reaction. Unfortunately, due to the lack of depth in this feedback, there are little to no changes that could be drawn from it
    (Unchanged)

  • 3.2.2
    This is valid feedback, however, for users with multiple cards, we don’t believe that it is possible for the payment flow to be reduced in steps, without the user being fully aware of what is happening at all times.
    (Unchanged)


Spend 3.3.x

Feedback



    Spend

  • 3.3.1
    The hierarchy of the spending page is wrong. It is suggested that the budget and available balance should go top, then card in the middle and then detailed transection in the lower section

  • 3.3.2
    As users scrolled up and down the recent transaction list, there were several who expressed maybe being able to view a bit more on the screen? Maybe perhaps the card that takes up quite a large bit of the screen becomes minimised to give more screen real estate for scrolling the transaction list?

  • 3.3.3
    There are two unexpected actions that occurred during the high fidelity testing. One was that tapping on a card in this spending screen took you to the pay screen for that chosen card. Two, tapping on the spending limit information in the spending screen took the user to the view budget option in the settings menu. Both of these actions were surprises to the testers, which might not be behaviour we want at this point in time?

Justification



    Spend

  • 3.3.1
    This is valid feedback, however, we realise that we have two elements on the screen with different interactions. We have a swiping card interaction that allows users to switch between each of their cards, and we also have a scrolling interaction for our recent transactions. Keeping these two interactions as far apart as possible greatly reduces any confusion between these two dimensions of interaction. I.e. If the transactions were directly below the card, users may start to be confused because the area for scrolling and swiping up and down are too close together and may result in unintentional interactions.
    (Unchanged)

  • 3.3.2
    There is information on the card that is fairly useless, and even more so for when the user is intentionally looking at other information like recent transactions. We believe it would be a good idea to implement this visual aid in helping users view more of the content that is important to them
    (Change made for High Fidelity V2)

  • 3.3.3
    Due to the unexpected surprise, we decided to remove this feature temporarily for version 4, since many of the users are fairly new to this application. We designed these features as shortcuts in mind, but it seems as though they may be ill-timed or prioritised in the design iterations too early
    (Change made for High Fidelity V2)


Track 3.4.x

Feedback



    Track

  • 3.4.1
    The investment section should have more detailed information. Even if it’s the prototype, the amount of information of Investment is too little in that investment usually contains complicated and many important information

  • 3.4.2
    It isn’t super obvious that the user can tap the tile to view more information

  • 3.4.3
    Could it be possible to have even greater flexibility with the graph, like scrolling horizontally on the graph shifts the x-axis, or pinch to zoom in or out?

Justification



    Track

  • 3.4.1
    Our investment tracking is built upon managed fund accounts. Unfortunately, there is a level of depth that viewing a self-managed fund requires that would greatly increase the complexity of the app design. Thus, in ideation, we found that presenting investment information through the use of managed funds aligns closer to the current depth of information that is provided by superannuation or personal banking. We understand that information like stock prices and volatility are incredibly important to self-managed funds, however, this is not something that application is currently designed to do.
    (Unchanged)

  • 3.4.2
    We need to make it clearer to users that they can tap to view more on tiles to see the graph view
    (Change made for High Fidelity V2)

  • 3.4.3
    This level of graph viewability could turn out to become a great feature of the app, but at the moment these functions are available with limitations. For zooming in and out, users can currently view the graph in different time periods, albeit set to daily, weekly, monthly or yearly; and they can also tap the left and right arrows to shift backwards or forwards through the data. Perhaps this advanced level of graph control could be considered for functionality beyond MVP
    (Backlog)


Notifications & Settings 3.5.x

Feedback



    Notifications & Settings

  • 3.5.1
    Some of the language in the settings page could be clearer to align better with the user’s expectations. ‘Set new budget’ implies that the user is creating a new budget, not editing the current one that has been set

  • 3.5.2
    One user struggled a bit when asked to find changing their budget which was under the settings page. Interesting, they did not enter the settings page until close to the end because they felt that settings was more for profile management

Justification



    Notifications & Settings

  • 3.5.1
    The current language for this doesn’t accurately reflect what the user should be expecting, and hence should change to ‘Change current budget’
    (Change made for High Fidelity V2)

  • 3.5.2
    While observing this, we believe this was a combination of nerves and an unintentional task flow given to the tester. We had unknowingly been going through the menus sequentially, and hence the user assumed we would just be moving on to something in the track screen. However, we cannot disregard this observation as the user did not mentally connect ‘budgeting’ to ‘settings’. Rather, as they continued to think aloud, they were trying to associate the word ‘Budgeting’ to something they might recognise in the Track screen. This could possibly be a one-off outlier.
    (Unchanged)


Budgeting 3.6.x

Feedback



    Budgeting

  • 3.6.1
    Maybe when you are setting the budget, could there be a slider instead of a normal text input?

  • 3.6.2
    When you put in a number for a specific category that adds to over the set overall budget, should that be allowed? If it is, it should have a warning alert where you have to confirm it

  • 3.6.3
    Include the overall budget value in the budget categories page so that the user can always reference back to the amount that they need to budget within

  • 3.6.4
    Need confirmation screens for saving changes to the budget

Justification



    Budgeting

  • 3.6.1
    We initially considered using sliders for the budget categories, however, we ran into a few issues. One, how many sliders are going to be on the screen at one time? Two, how do you determine the end point of the slider dynamically? Three, would a slider give us enough precision, especially when some budgets may require values beyond $1000 or $2000? Due to the complication of sliders on a touch screen, we don’t believe it would be the most appropriate way of providing this input
    (Unchanged)

  • 3.6.2
    At this point in time, we don’t feel like we should add to the restrictive nature of budgeting for our users, and hence we’d like to allow them to add budgets that go beyond the budget total. However, it is important to provide a level of system transparency to the user, and having a confirmation prompt to warn the user would be very helpful
    (Change made for High Fidelity V2)

  • 3.6.3
    This is something we would like to implement, but it raised questions about screen real estate. For the budget categories screen, we may need more screen real estate to state the ‘siloed’ nature of this customizability - after all, it is an option for the user to reach if they turn it on. However, there are still possible ways of adding this information in and will be considered
    (Backlog)

  • 3.6.4
    We will definitely need to have this feature to ensure that users are aware that they are making changes to their budget
    (Change made for High Fidelity V2)


High Fidelity Prototype Version 1: Reflection


Overall, there is still some work to do, especially in terms of confirming the overall colour scheme. It is clear that there is quite a large backlog of new features that could be added, but have unfortunately been sidelined to ensure that we can make improvements to the key features.
